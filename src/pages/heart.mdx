# Your heart runs on a lifetime, not a decade

Atherosclerosis is driven by **ApoB**, the number of atherogenic particles in
your blood, accumulated over *time*. The damage is the area under the curve:
particle concentration multiplied by years of exposure <Citation id="easLdlCausality2017" />.

Lowering ApoB earlier shrinks the area dramatically. That's why a 10-year risk
score, which under-weights younger people, misses the point for prevention.

## We have seen this before: blood pressure

We have seen this before, with blood pressure. Life-insurance actuaries quantified
that higher blood pressure predicted earlier death back in the 1920s, yet for decades
mainstream medicine treated moderately elevated pressure as benign "essential"
hypertension <Citation id="hypertensionHistory2011" />. Only after landmark trials
(and, much later, trials of *lower* targets like SPRINT <Citation id="sprint2015" />)
did guideline thresholds fall toward what the mortality data had shown all along: the
2017 ACC/AHA guideline finally moved "high blood pressure" down to 130/80
<Citation id="accAhaBp2017" />. The arc kept going: the 2025 ACC/AHA guideline now
urges getting *as close to under 120/80 as tolerated* <Citation id="accAhaBp2025" />,
because the intensive-target trials (SPRINT and the meta-analyses that followed) keep
showing fewer events at the lower end. ApoB looks like the same story, earlier in its arc.

## Where does your ApoB sit?

ApoB is a better measure of risk than LDL-C alone <Citation id="snidermanApoB2019" />.
Prevention-optimal targets are lower than population "normal", and there is no known
floor: in FOURIER, driving LDL-C down to under 20 mg/dL kept cutting events with no
threshold of benefit and no rise in adverse events <Citation id="fourierVeryLowLdl2017" />.
That "lower is better, all the way down" evidence is from high-risk *secondary*-prevention
patients, so it argues for *as low as reasonably achievable* rather than a fixed target of
20 for everyone; either way, the "Optimal" band below stays open-ended toward zero.

## Blood pressure

High blood pressure compounds ApoB-driven risk by accelerating arterial damage.
The 2017 ACC/AHA guideline lowered the thresholds, and "normal" is now under 120/80
<Citation id="accAhaBp2017" />. The `130/80` below is the *diagnostic* line where Stage 1
hypertension begins, not a prevention target. The prevention-optimal goal is to aim for
systolic **under 120 if you tolerate it** <Citation id="accAhaBp2025" />, which is the low
end the trials actually tested; there is no outcome evidence for routinely chasing 110, and
adverse events (dizziness, falls, kidney strain) climb as you push below that range. Because
office readings run high, judge yourself against *home* readings, rested, seated, and
averaged over several days.

## 10-year risk, in context

Conventional scores estimate your *next 10 years*. The AHA **PREVENT** model
(race-free) is the current standard <Citation id="preventEquations2024" />. Useful,
but it systematically under-weights younger people, which is exactly why the
lifetime exposure above matters more for prevention.
