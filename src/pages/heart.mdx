# Your heart runs on a lifetime, not a decade

Atherosclerosis is driven by **ApoB**, the number of atherogenic particles in
your blood, accumulated over *time*. The damage is the area under the curve:
particle concentration multiplied by years of exposure <Citation id="easLdlCausality2017" />.

## We have seen this before: blood pressure

We have seen this before, with blood pressure. Life-insurance actuaries quantified
that higher blood pressure predicted earlier death back in the 1920s, yet for decades
mainstream medicine treated moderately elevated pressure as benign "essential"
hypertension <Citation id="hypertensionHistory2011" />. Only after landmark trials
(and, much later, trials of *lower* targets like SPRINT <Citation id="sprint2015" />)
did guideline thresholds fall toward what the mortality data had shown all along: the
2017 ACC/AHA guideline finally moved "high blood pressure" down to 130/80
<Citation id="accAhaBp2017" />. ApoB looks like the same story, earlier in its arc.

## 10-year risk, in context

Conventional scores estimate your *next 10 years*. The AHA **PREVENT** model
(race-free) is the current standard <Citation id="preventEquations2024" />. Useful,
but it systematically under-weights younger people, which is exactly why the
lifetime exposure above matters more for prevention.
